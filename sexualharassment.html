Sexual Harassment

Every 98 seconds, an American is sexually assualted. Sexual harassment includes requests for sexual favors, advances, or other conducts either through physical or verbal action. It can also include offensive remarks about someone’s gender. The victim can be either a woman or man.

Statistics:

  • 1 out of every 6 American women has been the victim of an attempted or completed rape in her lifetime (14.8% completed, 2.8% attempted).
  • About 3% of American men have experienced an attempted or completed rape in their lifetime.
  • A majority of child victims are 12-17. Of victims under the age of 18: 34% of victims of sexual assault and rape are under age 12, and 66% of victims of sexual assault and rape are age 12-17
  • When the crime happened, 48% were sleeping, 29% were traveling to and from work or school, 12% were working, 7% were attending school, and 5% were doing an unknown or other activity.
  • 94% of women who are raped experience post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms in the two weeks following the assault.

Sexual harassment in the workplace:

1 in 3 women report that they have been sexually harassed in their workplace.
    What to do:
    • Report any cases and write down the situation exactly as it happened.
    • Talk to someone you can trust.
    • If it doesn’t get better, look for a different job.

How to cope:

  • It’s not your fault!
  • Think positively!
  • Do things you have always enjoyed to help take your mind off of it
  • Take your time
  • Talk to a trusted friend
  • Seek professional help. It's confidential!
